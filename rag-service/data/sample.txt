# PetHub Knowledge Base - Pet Care Guide

## Vaccination Guide

### Dogs
Puppies should receive their first vaccinations at 6-8 weeks of age. Core vaccines include:
- DHPP (Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvo, Parainfluenza)
- Rabies (required by law in most places)
- Bordetella (kennel cough) - recommended
- Leptospirosis - recommended

Vaccination schedule: 8 weeks (First DHPP), 12 weeks (Second DHPP + Rabies), 16 weeks (Third DHPP), then annual boosters. Adult dogs need rabies booster every 1-3 years depending on local law.

### Cats
Kittens need FVRCP (Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, Panleukopenia), Rabies, and FeLV (Leukemia) for outdoor cats.
Schedule: 8 weeks (First FVRCP), 12 weeks (Second FVRCP + Rabies), 16 weeks (Third FVRCP), then boosters annually or per vet recommendation.

## Nutrition & Feeding

### Dogs
Feed high-quality, balanced diet appropriate for age and size. Adults: 1-2 times daily. Puppies: 3-4 times daily.
Foods to avoid: chocolate, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic, xylitol, avocado.
Always provide fresh water.

Large breed puppies need special formula for slower growth. Senior dogs benefit from lower calories and joint support.

### Cats
Cats are obligate carnivores - need meat-based protein. Feed 1-2 times daily for adults, 3-4 times for kittens.
Foods to avoid: chocolate, onions, garlic, grapes, xylitol, raw fish.
Indoor cats often need portion control as obesity is common.

## Exercise & Activity

### Dogs
- Puppies: 5 minutes per month of age, 2-3 times daily
- Adults: 30 minutes to 2 hours daily depending on breed
- Herding breeds: 60-90 minutes high-intensity
- Senior dogs: 20-30 minutes daily, low-impact

### Cats
- Interactive play: 15-20 minutes daily minimum
- Enrichment: Climbing trees, window perches, puzzle feeders
- Rotate toys to maintain interest
- 2-3 play sessions daily for best behavior

## Grooming & Hygiene

### Dogs
- Brushing: 2-3 times weekly (daily for long-haired)
- Bathing: Monthly with dog shampoo
- Nail trimming: Every 4-6 weeks
- Ear cleaning: Weekly, especially for floppy-eared breeds
- Dental: Brush 3-5 times weekly; professional cleaning annually

### Cats
- Long-haired: Brush 2-3 times weekly
- Short-haired: Brush once weekly
- Nail trimming: Every 4 weeks
- Dental: Brush 3-5 times weekly
- Most cats self-groom; help with mats in long-haired cats

## Dental Health

Dental disease affects 90% of pets over age 4 and can lead to tooth loss, infections, and organ disease.

Prevention: Daily brushing (gold standard), dental diet, dental treats, professional cleaning annually.
Signs of disease: Bad breath, tartar buildup, bleeding gums, loose teeth, difficulty eating, jaw swelling.

## Common Health Issues

### Fleas & Ticks
Use monthly preventative medication (oral or topical). Products include Bravecto, NexGard, Simparica for dogs and Revolution/Capstar for cats.
Treat house and yard as needed with vet guidance.

### Urinary Tract Infections (UTI)
Signs: Frequent urination, difficulty urinating, blood in urine, straining, indoor accidents.
Prevention: Plenty of water, regular bathroom breaks, canned food for cats (higher moisture), good hygiene.
Treatment: Antibiotics from vet, pain management, increased water intake.

### Allergies
Can be food or environmental. Signs include itching, red skin, hair loss, ear infections, vomiting or diarrhea.
Management: Elimination diet (for food), environmental control, antihistamines or corticosteroids (vet prescribed), Omega-3 supplements.

### Obesity
Affects 55-60% of pets and increases risk of diabetes, joint problems, heart disease, and shortens lifespan.
Management: Portion control, measure food, increase exercise, replace treats with carrots or green beans, regular weight monitoring.

## Age-Related Care

### Puppies (0-1 year)
Frequent feeding (3-4 times daily), socialization critical from 3-12 weeks, positive training, vaccinations, deworming, microchipping, spay/neuter around 6 months.

### Adult Dogs (1-7 years)
Feed 1-2 times daily, exercise per breed guidelines, annual vet exams, vaccines, brush teeth regularly, annual dental cleaning.

### Senior Dogs (7+ years)
Biannual vet exams, senior diet formula, lower-impact exercise, joint supplements, orthopedic bed, monitor for cognitive decline.

### Senior Cats (7+ years)
Biannual exams, senior formula diet, kidney-friendly if needed, multiple water sources (consider fountain), easy-access litter boxes, monitor for kidney disease, thyroid disease, diabetes.

## Emergency Warning Signs - Seek Immediate Care For:
- Difficulty breathing
- Loss of consciousness
- Severe bleeding
- Seizures
- Suspected poisoning
- Uncontrolled vomiting/diarrhea
- Inability to urinate/defecate
- Extreme lethargy or signs of pain
- Pale gums
- Suspected trauma

## Regular Veterinary Care Schedule

Puppies & Kittens: Every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks (vaccines + exams)
Adult Pets (1-7 years): Annual wellness exam, annual vaccines, annual blood work at 5+ years
Senior Pets (7+ years): Biannual exams, biannual blood work, biannual urinalysis, dental cleaning as needed

## Important Disclaimer

This information is for educational purposes only and should not replace professional veterinary advice. Always consult a licensed veterinarian for your pet's medical needs. Pet care requires regular vet exams, preventative care, good nutrition, exercise, dental care, and love.
